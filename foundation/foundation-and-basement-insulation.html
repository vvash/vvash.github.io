Foundation and basement insulation

Adjoining the floor to the wall is a rather narrow place. here, significant heat leakage through the cold bridges with the participation of the foundation is possible. Therefore, it is important to determine these bridges of cold and qualitatively warm them.

Basement types:

Belt foundation

The most common variant of the basement. But it concerns and the most common mistake is warming, where the foundation is a bridge of cold. Often this problem is tried to solve with the help of a vertical insulation sheet. But this, as a rule, leads to the appearance of a crack on the floor covering over t he heater. This is due to the uneven subsidence of the floor screed and the insulation, foundation.

Pile foundation

With such a foundation, as a rule, sex is made on logs. In this case, lags are warmed, and the underground remains cold. As a heater is best to use mineral wool - it can be packed very tightly, while at the price it will be one of the most budgetary options.

The thickness of mineral wool should be 30 cm, if it is insulated only by beams, and 25 cm, if the beams and logs are insulated. The beams in terms of thermal conductivity are an order of magnitude worse than a heater, so they are a bridge of cold. In the case of thermal insulation and beams and logs, cold bridges are covered with a heater.

It is also important to provide good ventilation for the underfloor to dry beams and insulation.

Warming of the floor above the basement

The thickness of the insulation must be less than for the floor on Soil, as the temperature in the basement is higher than on the street.

Plate foundation

Walls are put on a solid plate. She is also fitted with a heater under the warm floor. If instead of a warm floor radiators will be used, the heater still has to be used.

"Swedish plate" foundation

This type of foundation is insulated from the very beginning, so it does not need additional insulation.
