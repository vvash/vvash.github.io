Wood protection

Modern technological processes of wood processing allow to conduct all work to protect wooden products in industrial conditions. But such products are more expensive and is still in low demand. Therefore, we will consider how to protect ourselves wooden structures of our house.

Protection of wooden structures begins already at the stage of acquiring wood for construction. Always need to buy well-dried wooden products. Presence in them the moisture content should not exceed 15-20% - this kind of wood is characterized by high strength. It is much less wrinkled, is not susceptible to rotting and cracking.

Pre-drying

Often, lumber is stored for a long time in the open air and gain excess moisture. therefore, before use, they must be dried. Natural drying lasts from several weeks to several months. To dry, any flat area that you need to cover with ruberoid. A layer of sawdust is filled from above. The lumber is laid on supports height 40-50 cm and shifted by wooden gaskets thickness of 15-20 mm. Stack width - 1.2 m (maximum), optimally - 0.8 m. The height can be any. When stacking the stack must take into account the direction of the wind. The stack of wood is laid across, not along the wind flow. To ensure that the wood does not crumble when drying, the top is stacked on top of the stack something heavy or tighten the ends with a rubber band 40 mm wide, which can be cut from an old car camera. If the pile is not located under a canopy, then it must be topped cover from the rain.

Treatment with protective compounds

Only after drying can one begin to work on wood with various compounds that protect it from fire, UV rays and biological pests. Such protection, as a rule, consists of from two layers of a primer, which is applied to a clean tree, and a film-forming composition in as the finish coat.

Paints

One of the most popular finish coatings is paint. Cover the wood with acrylic water-based paints or oil paints. Depending on the basis of the paint is selected primer. Apply paint along the wood fibers. Keep wet that area, with which you are currently working on, and do not allow overlapping with those areas that are dried. One single layer, updated every 1-2 years, will save the tree and provide an acceptable appearance of wooden structures. So, the paint reliably protects wood, such a coating durable, but it completely hides the texture of the wood. If you plan to save unique pattern, it is necessary to apply transparent materials, such as lacquer, azure, impregnation and oil.

Semi-transparent compounds have protective properties to a much greater extent than paints. First, the content of coloring pigments in them is an order of magnitude smaller, which allows them much it is better to be absorbed into the tree, protecting it from atmospheric influences "from within"; Secondly, in they contain much more special additives (fungicides, etc.) that prevent decay and destruction of a tree.

Impregnation

In modern construction for the protection of wood more and more prefer various impregnations. As a rule, it is a multicomponent composition that creates a reliable preventive protection of a healthy tree, protecting it from the appearance of fungus, mold, insects and blue. Impregnations penetrate deep into the wood, creating a protective layer. Also impregnations shade the pattern of wood and make its surface matte. Usually for full protection is sufficient for one layer of impregnation.

When using impregnation, it is necessary to strictly follow the instructions for use, as well as not use impregnation intended for external use, to protect internal wooden house designs. Impregnation should be applied with a soft brush, but not with a roller. Roller only splashes the mixture and does not give an even layer. Apply the impregnation on a clean dry the tree is fast enough. Each plane must be covered continuously, otherwise, if impregnation in this area will dry earlier than on the next, this place will turn out conspicuous seam. But you need to do this carefully, so as not to leave untreated areas. Apply impregnation in the direction of the wood fibers. Try not to take on the brush too a lot of make-up and smear random drops before they dry out.

Lacquers

To protect the wood and create a glossy surface, varnishes are used.

Lacquer must necessarily be applied along the fibers of wood, although this material can not too care about the accuracy of the application. Since most varnishes are absolutely transparent, during the application, it is easy not to notice the untreated areas.

To ensure uniform coverage of the surface, a good illumination and conduct regular inspections of treated areas. Smoothly work with a brush, so that the varnish layer is uniform. Wipe the first coat of varnish, as some types of this coating, especially water-based, have the property of spreading along the fibers of wood. Wipe the surface with a damp cloth to wipe off the dust, and allow the varnish to dry 2-3 hours before applying the next layer.

Antiseptic solutions

Despite the fact that the majority of impregnations include antiseptics and fungicides, preventing the development of rot and insect damage, some wooden elements (pillars, lags, etc.) that will be in contact with the ground or concrete, require additional protection with special antiseptic solutions. Antiseptics are colorless (sometimes with a weak amber or greenish tint) fungicidal solution, preventing rotting. The main task of such materials is to get as far into the tree as possible, therefore they always have a very liquid consistency. Surface treatment is done with brushes or a spray gun in 2-3 layers. Deeper impregnation can be obtained by immersing the products first in a hot (t = 90-95 ° C), and then in a cold (t = 20-30 ° C) bath with an antiseptic: in a hot antiseptics from the pores of the wood, excess air escapes, and when immersed in a cold bath in pores a vacuum is formed and the antiseptic impregnates the wood to a great depth. Such a impregnation is simply necessary for wood that will be in the ground.

The time of impregnation differs for different types of wood. The time and flow rate of the solution given in the instructions, as a rule, refer to easily impregnated wood. For guard moderately impregnated wood, these parameters should be increased by 1.5, and for slightly impregnated - 2 times. Wax and oil

Modern chemical protection of wood is, of course, good, but what about those who oppose use of chemistry even in a residential building. And here the natural materials: oil and wax.

Probably, linseed oil is more suitable for natural wood oils. For processing it is better to use boiled or twice boiled oil, as it dries more quickly and does not so sticky as raw. Apply the oil abundantly, in the direction of the fibers, a little like rubbing it with a soft brush to ensure full coverage and penetration into the surface. Allow the oil to absorb a little, and then remove the excess with a piece tissue. Leave the treated wood for half an hour, then polish the surface dry rag. After oil treatment, the wood pattern becomes particularly saturated and, as it were, somewhat damp. You can leave the coating in this form, and you can still work on top waxing. To do this, you need to prepare a wax mastic.

The simplest wax mastic is prepared from wax and turpentine in a ratio of 2: 1. We take 100 grams wax, 25 g of ground rosin and 50 g of purified turpentine.

In a water bath, melting the wax in an enameled container, add rosin. After dissolving the wax gradually add turpentine. We remove everything from the fire, we pour it into a tin a jar and let the composition cool down. Mastic becomes thick and pasty.

Mastic is applied to the wood with a piece of cloth. It does not have to be applied in the direction fibers, but this method is still the most effective in finishing wood surfaces. The tissue is taken a small amount of mastic and rubbed into the tree, until will remain the finishing material. After this, the treated area is given a little dry, but do not freeze, after which it is polished with a dry, clean cloth.
