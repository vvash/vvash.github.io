Types of slate roofs

                        

The most common variants of slate roofs have been and remain wavy sheets to                             To add to them the attractiveness, manufacturers began to paint this material in different                             colors. However, high results have not yet been achieved in this area. Organic paints                             Are less durable than the material itself, which can serve 30-40 years. AT                             Currently, work is under way to create inorganic paints that will serve                             As much as the slate itself.

Asbestos cement wavy sheet - this is a roofing made of finely-reinforced                             Cement stone, the fibers of which have high tensile strength, are perceived                             Tensile stresses. Such material possesses not only high mechanical strength,                             But also other valuable properties: fire resistance, low water permeability, durability,                             Has a small mass, 2-3 times cheaper than a metal roof.

                        

Asbestos-cement roofs from corrugated sheets of an ordinary profile are arranged on roofs with                             A slope of more than 20% (the optimal slope of the roof is 20-45 °).

                        

Before starting work, the sheets are sorted, carefully examined and parallel                             Prepare the steel elements of the roof (cornices, gutters, etc.).

                        

Than good asbestos-cement roofing

                        

Slate has high water-repellent properties, which is a very important indicator                             For roofing material. In addition, he stoically exerts the influence of high and low                             Temperature, humidity, solar radiation and snow loads, with the weight                             Material is low, which makes it possible to mount a lightweight and sparse                             Crate.

                        

Asbestos-cement has heat-insulating properties, under it moisture does not condense,                             So there is no need for vapor barrier. It effectively extinguishes noise from atmospheric precipitation.                             Asbestos-cement slate does not burn, and its cost can be safely attributed to the category                             Pluses.

                        

Uncomplicated this material and in the processing: it can be cut and drilled with ordinary carpentry                             Tools.

                        

Disadvantages of asbestos-cement roofing

                        

At the end of its life, the asbestos-cement roof begins to degrade actively: it loses its                             Waterproof qualities, swells, and the edges of the sheets are easily dyed and broken off                             (Especially in the longitudinal direction in asbestos-cement wavy sheets), but similar phenomena                             Are observed on the roofs, which are 50 years old.

                        

What really is the scourge of every slate roof is moss and lichens,                             Who willingly settle down from the north side of the house. Periodically, they need to be cleaned.

                        

Bundle material

                        

Boards and bars for the crate should be taken not less than the second grade, width - no more than 14 cm,                             Well dried (raw wood during drying will loosen the nail joint). If                             The use of sawn timber is wider than 14 cm, in the end they will shrivel, warp and                             Will damage the roof. Also it is desirable to choose boards without knots, they can break at                             Load.

                        

The size of the bar of the crate must be a section of 50-50 or 60-60 mm. The lining is nailed to                             Rafters with nails. Lay out and fix the bars of the battens from the eaves to the ridge in steps of 530                             Mm.

                        

Each slate sheet must be supported by only three bars: at the edges and in the middle. At such a                             The longitudinal overlap of the slate sheets will be dense and they will be firmly                             Bar.

The roof roof is designed so that it can be packed with an integer                             Sheets in both longitudinal and transverse directions. If this is not possible, in the roof                             Insert cut sheets that are laid in transverse rows next to the penultimate of the pediment                             Overhang, and in the longitudinal - at the ridge.

                        

Using cropped pieces is not very convenient and cost-effective, which is more correct                             Increase (but not more than 50 cm) or in extreme cases reduce the overhangs on the gables.

                        

All even bars with the correct approach should have a height of not 60 mm, but 63 mm (in the case                             50х50 - the height is not 50 mm, but 53 mm). For uniformity they are usually made the same, then                             There is 60x60 mm (or 50x50), but in this case even bars are raised with the aid of bed                             Laths 3 mm thick, laid on rafters. All these conditions must be observed,                             So that the overlap of the sheets is dense and the sheets are firmly laid on the crate.

                        

We lay the slate sheet

                        

In order for the sheets to lie flat, you must use a cord stretched along the ramp.                             The horizontal lapping of the rows should be 140 mm at a slope of up to 30-58% and 120 mm at more                             Steep incline, and it is desirable to take into account the direction of the prevailing winds in this                             Terrain so that the open edges of the sheets are facing the leeward side.

                        

Cover the roof with overlapping one sheet of another one wave (with a dense overlap)                             Asbestos-cement sheets can be in two ways: with the displacement of sheets in each subsequent row                             And with cutting off adjacent angles.

                        

According to the first method, the slate sheets are laid unzipped - with the longitudinal edges of the sheets displaced                             On one or several waves in relation to the same edges of the sheets of the previously stacked row. AT                             In this case, to cover one roof slope, it is likely to have to be cut along only                             One sheet of slate either on one wave, or the whole sheet in half (both parts of you                             Will need the next ramp).

                        

As a result, undesirable four-fold overlapping of sheets in places of common                             Joints, and the sheets themselves fit without cracks.

                        

According to the second method, only corners (one wave) are cut in sheets, then the line for joining sheets                             On the slope along the longitudinal edges will be straight.

It is recommended to cover relatively narrow sloping sheets with relatively narrow slopes, but                             Long in the transverse direction of the ramp. Wide in slope, but short in transverse                             The slope direction is covered with sheets with cut corners.

                        

When laying asbestos-cement sheets, the four corners should not converge in one place?                             Will lead to a distortion of the sheets and their loose fit. Snow and wind load on                             Sheets will cause their excessive deformation, contributing to the development of cracks. Under tight joints                             Begins to blow the wind, bringing snow to the attic. In this case it is necessary to put under                             Slate roofing paper, which, in turn, causes the need for a continuous crate, and you                             Get a more expensive roof.

                        

If there are still gaps of more than 7 mm between the slate sheets, they should be closed with                             Using waterproofing mastic, which is applied to overlapping strips of wood                             Spatula with a layer 5-6 mm thick, with a width of 30-40 mm in the transverse joints, and in longitudinal -                             60-70 mm.

                        

Ways to fasten the slate to the crate

                        

The hole for fixing the slate is made at a distance of 80-100 mm from the bottom edge of the ridge                             Waves with a hand drill (the diameter of the drill is 1-2 mm larger than the diameter of the nail). If you do not take into account these                             Proportions, then during mechanical and temperature deformations the sheet will crack. Wherein                             Use a template to prevent the drill from slipping. The position of the holes depends on the intervals                             Between beams of the crate. The layout of the locations of future holes is carried out when the sheets are still                             Are in the original packaging.

                        

The sheet is fastened to the crate with a slate (or usual) nail or screws 100 mm long with washer                             From metal and a lining from rubber, roofing felt, a roofing material which cover a paint composition                             (Preferably a primer) on natural linseed oil or mastic; The nail is slaughtered until                             Washers will not perform the rest of the paint (not finishing to the end about 2-3 mm).

                        

Unlike other building materials, the slate does not have additional elements. Therefore, the                             In such a roof are protected by roofing steel or simply by two edged boards knocked down between                             Yourself at the right angle.

                        

Repair of asbestos-cement roofs

If you are at the inspection of the roof found heavily damaged sheets of slate (there are chipped,                             Cracks) prepare for the fact that they will have to be replaced. For this, in the longitudinal                             Direction from both sides of the removed sheet reinforce the bridges behind the ridge brackets. Across                             Bridges lay the board. To release the damaged sheet, remove nails or screws,                             Which it is attached to the crate. When pulling nails, the claw's feet rest on the edge                             Navigation bridge or board. The damaged sheet is removed from the row so that the adjacent sheet                             Remained in place. A new sheet is laid by two roofer. One lifts the weakened side                             And on top of the sheets, and another, laying a new asbestos-cement sheet on the overlapping edge                             Next, moves it towards the ridge. When the bottom edge of the new sheet coincides                             With the edge of this row, it is attached to the base. Soft washers in all attachments lubricate and                             They are stained with a surreal paint.

                        

Asbestos sheets themselves are subject to repair, however, the damage should not be significant.                             Small cracks can be pasted with tar, underlay felt, mastic cloth, copying                             The surface of the sheet. Pasted sheet manually. Apply a brush to the corrugations with mastic, then                             Paste the fabric, pressing it firmly against the surface of the sheet.

                        

When replacing up to 10 m2 of the cover from asbestos-cement sheets, first disassemble the overlying                             Roofing, then remove the damaged sheets. New sheets are laid on the crate                             From bottom to top, that is, from the eaves to the ridge with overlapping of the underlying row by 120 mm with                             Slope of more than 58% and 140 mm with a smaller slope. The sheets are fastened to the crate by one nail or                             Screw, necessarily putting under his head a soft washer. Sheets of cornice support                             Additionally with two screws.

                        

Painting of asbestos-cement roofing

                        

Painted slate roofs look quite respectable, and paint is optional                             Protection for the material, prolonging his life, including not allowing lichens and mosses                             Settle on this roofing. Paint reduces water absorption, increases                             Frost resistance of roofing materials and prolongs their service life.

                        

For asbestos-cement roofing, weatherproof, moisture-resistant and frost-resistant                             Oil and acryl paints and enamels.

                        

A new slate before painting is recommended to withstand                             Atmospheric conditions for 3-4 months.

                        

When painting old roofs, the vegetation on them should be removed mechanically from                             Using metal brushes or scrapers, then the surface should be etched with antiseptic                             And rinse with water.

Before proceeding with painting, the slate is primed and allowed to dry. This will fix                             Surface and will prevent further color separation.

                        

Asbestos-cement roofing is painted with a fly brush or a hand, collecting a small amount                             Paint and shading it along the slope. The duration of each layer                             Temperature of air 10-15 ° С not less than 24-36 hours.

                        

With sheets covered with paint, handle carefully, so as not to damage the finishing layer. AT                             The first years of operation at home a layer of paint after attaching the roof is usually slightly erased.                             This is especially noticeable when snow falls from the roof, it can have a shade of roofing                             Material, but that's okay.

                        

Asbestos-cement sheets should in no case be punched with nails. So you get instead                             Neat hole network of cracks, which with time will turn into the launching pad for                             More serious defects. Given that in Belarus several times a year is announced                             Storm warning, slate must be fastened only with screws and staples in the cornice                             Row.
