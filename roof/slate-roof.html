Slate roof

During the rather aggressive seizure of the market for new roofing materials slate, as a cheap material and the most common, underwent active he was accused of all deadly sins, clearly hinting that such material is no longer a place on our roofs.

Asbestos as a material is harmful to the body, but not everything is as bad as loudly about this is shouted.

What is slate?

Slate is a roofing material, and from it kitchen tables are not is going to. We are separated from it by several layers of roofing carpet, as we see, it does not is in direct contact with a person and is unlikely to cause harm to health.

By itself, asbestos is harmful just as bad as any fiber of organic and of inorganic origin. If we eat it, nothing will happen, but if day to day we will inhale a huge number of long and thin fibers, they will accumulate in the lungs and will cause harm to health, but such a dangerous concentration is possible only in the places of extraction or dry processing of asbestos, and even if there is compliance with all safety standards, avoid fatal consequences.

And, the Americans found out that in a house covered with asbestos slate and during construction which used other asbestos containing materials, a dust concentration of less than 0.001 fiber in 1 cm3, which is 1000 times less than the threshold threshold value.

Types of slate roofs

The most common variants of slate roofs have been and remain wavy sheets to to add to them the attractiveness, manufacturers began to paint this material in different colors. However, high results have not yet been achieved in this area. Organic paints are less durable than the material itself, which can serve 30-40 years. AT currently, work is under way to create inorganic paints that will serve as much as the slate itself.

Asbestos cement wavy sheet - this is a roofing made of finely reinforced cement stone, the fibers of which have high tensile strength, are perceived tensile stresses. Such material possesses not only high mechanical strength, but also other valuable properties: fire resistance, low water permeability, durability, has a small mass, 2-3 times cheaper than a metal roof.

Asbestos-cement roofs from corrugated sheets of an ordinary profile are arranged on roofs with a slope of more than 20% (the optimal slope of the roof is 20-45 °).

Before starting work, the sheets are sorted, carefully inspected and parallel prepare the steel elements of the roof (cornices, gutters, etc.).

Slate has high water-repellent properties, which is a very important indicator for roofing material. In addition, he stoically exerts the influence of high and low temperature, humidity, solar radiation and snow loads, with the weight material is low, which makes it possible to mount a lightweight and sparse crate.

Asbestos-cement has heat-insulating properties, under it moisture does not condense, so there is no need for vapor barrier. It effectively extinguishes noise from atmospheric precipitation. asbestos-cement slate does not burn, and its cost can be safely attributed to the category pluses.

This material is also unfit for processing: it can be sawed and drilled with ordinary carpentry tools.

Disadvantages of asbestos-cement roofing

At the end of the life of the asbestos-cement roof begins to collapse: it loses its waterproof qualities, swells, and the edges of the sheets are easily dyed and broken off (Especially in the longitudinal direction in asbestos-cement wavy sheets), but similar phenomena are observed on the roofs, which are 50 years old.

But what really is the scourge of every slate roof is moss and lichen, who willingly settle down from the north side of the house. Periodically they need to be cleaned.
